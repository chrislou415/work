Country Covid-19 Vaccinations

Introduction

The New Coronavirus vaccine is highly effective in preventing illness, hospitalization, or death due to infection with New Coronavirus. Early evidence also suggests that the neo-coronavirus vaccine helps reduce the spread of neo-coronavirus. Medical evidence suggests that if a person becomes ill with a neo-coronavirus infection after vaccination, he or she is generally less likely to cause others to become ill compared to unvaccinated individuals.

With COVID-19 vaccination, the risk of acquiring a new coronavirus is reduced, and even if you do acquire a new coronavirus, you are less likely to develop severe disease, and your friends, family and community are protected. If most people are vaccinated, the new coronavirus is less likely to spread. This also protects people who cannot be vaccinated.

The heads of the International Monetary Fund, the World Bank Group, the World Health Organization, and the World Trade Organization held high-level consultations on March 1, 2022, with UNICEF, the Global Alliance for Vaccine Immunization, the Global Chief Coordinator for COVID-19 Vaccine Country Readiness and Delivery, and the chief executives of major vaccine manufacturers to ensure rapid delivery to where it is most needed and vaccination campaigns where they are most needed.
